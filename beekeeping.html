    how to become a beekeeper

    first you have to educate yourself on beekeeping, look for classes nearby your area and see if there are any schools that specialize in agriculture since they may host courses. you can also just read up on beekeeping or learn from a beekeeper yourself! california is especially abundant when it comes to the number of beekeepers there are.

    to purchase a hive you can get a "starter kit" that has all hive components you need to start beekeeping (available online and from farm supply stores), it should include: hive boxes (2/3), frames, bottom board, hive outer and inner cover, the hive stand, and bee feeders! you'll also need: a smoker and shield to make the bees docile, and a hive tool to take hives apart. lastly, you can get a bee brush to move them around without injuring them, and an extractor to harvest honey. make sure to get protective clothing, including: round tie-down veil, hard hat used with the veil, gloves, and a bee suit.

    you need to set up the hive in a place near flowers, and away from human traffic, and high winds (+ other crazy weathers). keep in mind that the types of flowers you have will affect the flavor of the honey!

    the easiest way to buy bees is to go to a beekeeper and purchase bees directly from them. there are 4 different types of honey bees: Italians, Caucasians, Carniolans, and hybrids. Italian bees are the most popular because of the amount they produce, but Caucasians are the gentlest and Carniolans can survive the winter well. make sure to purchase the bees in the spring!

    once you get your cute little bees, feed the new colonies until they're established. (nectar made of: 1 part sugar, 2 parts water; put this in the bee feeder or place it in the hive). give your bees lots of space to grow and buzz around, and give them lots of love and care.

    how to support your local beekeepers

    you can visit local beekeepers and find locally, organic honey at farmers markets in your area! the price may be more costly than store-bought (aka costco) honey, but you'll be supporting your local beekeeper and the quality of the honey will typically be higher!
