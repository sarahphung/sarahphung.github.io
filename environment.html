bees and the environment
    why bees are important to the environment

    bees are the most abundant pollinators, and more than 1/3 of the food we consume each day relies on pollination. from avocados, soybeans, asparagus, and sunflowers, to non-foods like cotton and flax. they support the growth of trees, flowers, and other plants which can be food/shelter for creatures of varying sizes.

    bees pollinate plants by transfering pollen from the male part of the flower (anther) to the female part (stigma); by doing this the plant's seed/nut/fruit is then formed. plants sometimes rely on bees and animals to assist with the pollination process, while others rely on wind. this is essential for plant reproduction!

    overall, they play a large role in aiding the flourishing biodiversity on our planet, wildlife habitats, wild plant growth, and more.

    how to support your local bees

    on hot days (or ones with unfavorable weather), be sure to leave some sugar water out for bees so they can gather their strength to continue pollinating everything!

    you can also plant things like: fennel, sunflowers, coneflowers, geraniums, sage, thyme, poppy, lavender, verbena, rosemary, aster, buttercup, and more. these herbs and flowers will do wonders to your garden and spice rack, and bees love them! for more plant ideas, check out this website: https://thehoneybeeconservancy.org/plant-a-bee-garden/.
