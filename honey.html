    how honey is made

    bees gather either pollen or nectar from flowers, and then store this in their special honey stomach to be transferred to the honey-making bees in the hive. if they get hungry on their trip, they can also tap into these nectar 'sacs' to convert it into energy.

    they proceed to carry the payload back to the hive, and then pass the nectar to other bees using mouth-to-mouth until its moisture content is reduced from 70 to 20%! the worker bees chew on it for about half an hour and it's continuously passed down from bee to bee until it turns to honey. the honey is placed into storage cells and capped with beeswax in readiness for the arrival of precious newborn baby bees! pollen is mixed with the nectar to make bee bread which is fed to the babies.

    how to support your local bees

    you can visit local beekeepers and find locally, organic honey at farmers markets in your area! the price may be more costly than store-bought (aka costco) honey, but you'll be supporting your local beekeeper and the quality of the honey will typically be higher!
